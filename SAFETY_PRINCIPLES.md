# Safety Principles

## Safety Position
Clarity.ai - transparent dating is a dating platform with elevated clarity and moderation needs, not a clinical service. Safety work should focus on consent, deception, harassment, coercion, exploitation, and platform misuse.

## Core Principles

### 1. Clarity beats ambiguity
Users should not have to guess the platform’s expectations around respectful behavior, reporting, or blocks.

### 2. No medical proof walls
The product must not require diagnosis documentation, clinical validation, or any proof of neurodivergence.

### 3. No diagnosis inference
The platform must not infer whether someone is “really” ADHD, autistic, AuDHD, or otherwise neurodivergent.

### 4. No clinical interpretation
AI or product copy must not present mental-health or psychological conclusions as truth.

### 5. Consent is operational, not decorative
Consent should appear in product structure:
- explicit profile fields
- messaging expectations
- report/block affordances
- moderation categories

### 6. Reporting must be taken seriously
Users need a direct route to report harassment, coercion, impersonation, fetishization, manipulation, or unsafe sexual conduct.

### 7. Blocks must be respected immediately
A blocked user should be removed from messaging and candidate retrieval surfaces without delay.

### 8. Privacy work needs real review
Deletion, retention, data export, age-gating, and legal language must receive real legal review before public launch.

## Launch Safety Focus Areas
- coercive or manipulative behavior
- harassment or repeated boundary violations
- fetishization of neurodivergence
- impersonation or deceptive identity claims
- sexually explicit abuse or unsafe escalation
- hate, bigotry, or demeaning language

## Moderation Posture For MVP
- manual review remains primary
- AI can assist triage, summarization, and categorization only
- no automated permanent enforcement without human review path
- logs should preserve enough context for founder review

## Product-Level Safety Design Choices
- no infinite swipe loop
- no streaks or compulsion mechanics
- explicit reporting and block flow
- structured onboarding that encourages expectation-setting
- profile and message objects designed for moderation metadata later

## High-Risk Areas Requiring Future Legal / Policy Review
- age verification approach
- privacy policy and consent copy
- data retention timelines
- DSAR / deletion workflow
- moderation appeals and account actions
- special-category data treatment under applicable law

## Safety Success For This Repo Run
- moderation categories exist
- report data model exists
- content policy draft exists
- review prompt/template exists
- safety assumptions and non-goals are documented honestly
