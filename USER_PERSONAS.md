# User Personas

## Persona 1: Lara, 31, AuDHD Product Designer

### Snapshot
- lives in Neukolln
- wants a long-term relationship
- highly articulate in writing, overloaded in chaotic social spaces
- uses mainstream apps but burns out from vague banter and ambiguous intentions

### What she needs
- explicit communication norms
- low-pressure conversation starters
- clarity on pacing and emotional availability
- ability to filter people who expect constant texting or spontaneous plans

### What breaks trust
- flirt-first interfaces with no substance
- profiles that hide intent
- “just go with the flow” messaging
- AI summaries that sound like therapy notes

## Persona 2: Jonas, 28, ADHD Software Engineer

### Snapshot
- lives in Friedrichshain
- open to dating, not sure whether he wants short-term or long-term yet
- warm, funny, and energetic, but inconsistent with replies when overloaded
- worries about looking flaky or careless

### What he needs
- room to explain response patterns without sounding defensive
- profile prompts that separate interest from response speed
- matching that accounts for pacing expectations
- reminders in product copy that clarity beats perfection

### What breaks trust
- punishment-by-silence social norms
- aggressive urgency cues
- features that reward constant checking

## Persona 3: Merve, 35, Autistic Researcher

### Snapshot
- lives in Charlottenburg
- wants a serious relationship
- deeply values specificity, predictability, and sensory compatibility
- often feels unseen on apps optimized for quick charisma

### What she needs
- strong profile structure
- ability to state sensory needs without stigma
- date format preferences surfaced early
- respectful filtering of neurotype compatibility

### What breaks trust
- vague labels such as “easygoing”
- pressure to infer tone or subtext
- systems that frame directness as coldness

## Persona 4: Alex, 33, International Creative With ADHD

### Snapshot
- lives in Kreuzberg
- bilingual, socially active, but exhausted by dating ambiguity
- interested in both English and German-speaking matches
- open to dating neurotypical users later but wants strict filters now

### What they need
- language preference visibility
- Berlin distance and logistics preferences
- explicit mutual interest around planning style and social energy
- protection from being treated as a novelty or educational exercise

### What breaks trust
- “teach me about your condition” behavior
- dating apps that treat neurodivergence as a gimmick
- pressure to constantly socialize outside capacity

## Shared Needs Across Launch Personas
- clarity without clinical framing
- explicit communication and sensory context
- predictable UI and pacing
- low-clutter onboarding
- credible safety posture

## Persona Design Implications
- structured inputs matter more than decorative profiles
- compatibility explanations should be short and concrete
- messaging should reduce guesswork, not add personality scoring
- settings need explicit filtering controls
- every important action needs a plain-language label
