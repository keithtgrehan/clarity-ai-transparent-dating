# Red lines

Stop implementation or release if a change would:

- infer, verify, score, or expose diagnosis, neurotype authenticity, severity, trauma, attachment, motive, deception, attraction, desirability, or hidden mental state;
- use diagnosis for service access, profile visibility, eligibility, ordering, or explanation;
- create a romance-success, compatibility-truth, attractiveness, popularity, activity, response-time, or engagement score;
- silently treat missing information as positive evidence;
- upload private content, reuse service data for research/training, or retain withdrawn data without an approved purpose and consent/lawful-basis path;
- put raw private messages, reports, diagnosis context, emails, transcripts, or research notes in logs, analytics, CI, match responses, public artifacts, or model prompts without an approved data flow;
- impose an autonomous moderation sanction or remove access to human review and appeal;
- use dark patterns, infinite swipe, streaks, artificial scarcity, urgency, guilt, or notification pressure;
- recruit participants before privacy, ethics, compensation, withdrawal, deletion, safeguarding, and incident procedures are approved;
- copy donor/external material without exact commit/blob provenance and rights approval.

When uncertain, fail closed for eligibility or release, preserve user protection, record the decision needed, and escalate to the accountable owner.
