# Canonical research status

Status: evidence synthesis for a synthetic, local research milestone only

Last reviewed: 2026-07-15

This document is the canonical interpretation layer for Clarity research. It does not authorize participant research, data collection, scraping, training, audio processing, deployment, or a model-quality, clinical, legal-compliance, relationship-outcome, or market claim. The literature search is broad but not exhaustive; fast-moving and under-indexed work may be missing.

## Product thesis supported by the current evidence

Clarity should help a person inspect **observable wording and structure**, decide what they want to communicate, and edit one low-pressure repair action. It should not “correct” a neurotype or rank a person. Relationship research makes responsiveness, listening, maintenance and repair more defensible design foundations than trait correction, while autism communication studies show that interaction outcomes depend on both people and the communication context, not a one-sided deficit ([Itzchakov, Reis & Weinstein, 2022](https://doi.org/10.1111/spc3.12648); [Ogolsky et al., 2023](https://doi.org/10.1111/pere.12429); [Crompton et al., 2020](https://doi.org/10.3389/fpsyg.2020.586171); [Morrison et al., 2020](https://doi.org/10.1177/1362361319892701)).

The current product direction is therefore:

1. user-selected communication tasks, never inferred diagnosis or “type”;
2. at most three evidence-backed wording/structure cues;
3. an explicit low-signal or unknown state when context is inadequate;
4. one editable repair action that preserves the other person’s response space;
5. no scalar compatibility, attraction, deception, personality or relationship-success score;
6. no use of pauses, pitch, lexical density or reply timing to infer emotion, cognition, neurotype or motive;
7. user-paced, finite interaction with no urgency, streaks, variable rewards, notification pressure or re-analysis pressure.

## Evidence-to-decision matrix

| Question | Evidence status | What the evidence permits | What remains blocked |
|---|---|---|---|
| Do autistic adults want and form romantic relationships? | Moderate observational evidence; heterogeneous samples | Design for varied relationship goals and preferences | Treating autism as disinterest, incapacity, or a dating score |
| What helps communication? | Moderate cross-neurotype and general-relationship evidence | Explicit preferences, high-quality listening, mutual responsiveness, clarification and repair | A single “normal” conversational style or one-sided correction |
| What does ADHD relationship research support? | Limited-to-moderate reviews, observational and qualitative studies | Voluntary structure, timing clarity, request splitting and repair support | Inferring ADHD, symptom severity, rejection sensitivity or relationship risk from text |
| What is known specifically about AuDHD dating? | Direct adult dating evidence remains sparse | Treat co-occurrence as a research gap and rely on user-selected needs | Extrapolating autism-only or ADHD-only findings into an AuDHD profile |
| Can pitch, pause or pacing infer emotion/neurotype? | Heterogeneous, task-, language- and sample-dependent evidence | Descriptive acoustic measurements in isolated research only | Emotion, cognitive-state, personality, diagnosis, attraction or compatibility inference |
| Are US/UK/Euro-English and code-switching covered? | No Clarity population validation | Preserve language/dialect as declared context and abstain where rules are unsupported | Dialect robustness or fairness claims; normalizing Euro-English toward US/UK English |
| Does identifier removal make text anonymous? | No; text anonymisation is imperfect and context can remain identifying | Data minimisation, layered detection, receipts and fail-closed rejection | Complete identifier-removal, anonymous, irreversible, compliant or risk-free claims |
| Can synthetic fixtures validate the product? | Useful for contracts and behavioral coverage only | Regression, hard-negative, low-signal, safety and accessibility tests | Real-world accuracy, subgroup fairness, market efficacy or participant-readiness claims |
| Can a matching system learn compatibility? | No approved corpus or outcome construct | Deterministic, user-chosen dimensions with provenance and unknown states | Outcome optimization, popularity ranking, inferred sensitive traits or a total fit score |

## Relationship and communication evidence

Autistic adults report diverse relationship interests and experiences. Strunz et al. found substantial relationship interest in a self-selected sample, and Byers et al. documented varied sexual and relationship experience; neither study supports predicting an individual’s preferences ([Strunz et al., 2017](https://doi.org/10.1002/jclp.22319); [Byers et al., 2013](https://doi.org/10.1177/1362361311431950)). A 2024 scoping review of autism and online dating found only eight studies from 2014–2023, generally with small convenience samples, and identified safety, stigma, controlled self-presentation and communication as recurring areas rather than settled effects ([Neumann et al., 2024](https://doi.org/10.1007/s40489-024-00488-5)). A 2025 content analysis similarly found that strengths are less studied than challenges and that dyadic and queer relationship evidence remains limited ([Neu et al., 2025](https://doi.org/10.1111/jftr.70001)).

Communication research supports a reciprocal account. Same-neurotype and mixed-neurotype interaction studies have produced context-dependent results, but consistently make a one-sided “autistic deficit” product framing untenable. Same-neurotype pairs sometimes report stronger rapport; autistic peer information transfer can be effective; and a larger 2025 multi-site diffusion-chain study found no material information-transfer disadvantage across matched and mixed chains while rapport varied ([Crompton et al., 2020a](https://doi.org/10.1177/1362361320919286); [Crompton et al., 2020b](https://doi.org/10.3389/fpsyg.2020.586171); [Crompton et al., 2025](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41562-025-02163-z); [Heasman & Gillespie, 2019](https://doi.org/10.1177/1362361318785172)). Long or information-dense turns must therefore be treated as a scanability condition only when a user asks for that support—not as pathology, low rapport, or a prediction about the listener.

Adult ADHD relationship research reports interpersonal strain in some samples, but effects vary by design, symptom grouping, partner and context. Reviews and qualitative studies support offering practical communication structure without turning those group findings into individual inference ([Wymbs et al., 2021](https://doi.org/10.1111/jmft.12475); [Ginapp et al., 2023](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ssmqr.2023.100223)). Conflict-task and self-report studies are not evidence that a text classifier can identify ADHD or predict relationship quality ([Canu et al., 2014](https://doi.org/10.1111/jmft.12018); [Sacchetti & Lefler, 2011](https://doi.org/10.1177/1087054709347257)). Soares et al. examined autism and ADHD symptom dimensions in romantic relationships, but this does not establish an AuDHD dating model or a valid matching variable ([Soares et al., 2021](https://doi.org/10.3389/fpsyt.2021.593150)).

General relationship evidence is more mature than diagnosis-specific dating evidence. High-quality listening and perceived responsiveness are plausible, trainable interaction processes, and a systematic review finds that relationship maintenance is a broad, multi-process construct rather than one magic behavior ([Itzchakov, Reis & Weinstein, 2022](https://doi.org/10.1111/spc3.12648); [Ogolsky et al., 2023](https://doi.org/10.1111/pere.12429)). “Repair” in Clarity means an observable attempt to reset, clarify, apologize, restate a boundary or agree a next step. It is not a claim that the relationship is healthy or will succeed; the direct repair literature is narrower and includes small or context-specific studies ([Gottman, Driver & Tabares, 2015](https://doi.org/10.1080/08975353.2015.1038962)).

## Acoustic, dialect and code-switch boundaries

Prosody research in autism is heterogeneous. A systematic review and meta-analysis found inconsistent measures and methods, and a cross-linguistic study found that some intonation results differed between English and Cantonese ([Fusaroli et al., 2017](https://doi.org/10.1002/aur.1678); [Zhang et al., 2022](https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0269637)). These papers study groups under specific tasks; they do not validate individual emotion or neurotype inference. Acoustic measurements, if separately approved later, may describe recording-level pace, silence and f0 summaries only, with explicit task, device, language, accent and uncertainty metadata.

Speech and text systems are known to vary across dialects and code-switching contexts. Commercial ASR error disparities and dialect-sensitive NLP performance make “cross-accent” or “Euro-English” support an evaluation obligation, not a feature claim ([Koenecke et al., 2020](https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.1915768117); [Tatman & Kasten, 2017](https://www.isca-archive.org/interspeech_2017/tatman17_interspeech.html); [Blodgett, Green & O’Connor, 2016](https://aclanthology.org/D16-1120/); [Aguilar et al., 2020](https://aclanthology.org/2020.lrec-1.223/)). Existing code-switch benchmarks cover selected language pairs, not Berlin Euro-English as a coherent validated dialect. Clarity must record `unknown` language support and abstain when a cue’s rule has not been tested for the declared context.

## Privacy, evidence and synthetic-data boundaries

Text anonymisation research documents persistent recall, context and re-identification limitations; replacing names, locations and handles in Clarity is data minimisation, not proof that data are anonymous ([Lison et al., 2021](https://aclanthology.org/2021.acl-long.323/); [Gadotti et al., 2024](https://doi.org/10.1126/sciadv.adn7053)). Health, sexuality and relationship text can reveal special-category or otherwise intimate information even after obvious identifiers are removed. The current system must therefore avoid persistence and provider transfer, emit only bounded receipts/metadata, and refuse complete identifier-removal, storage-erasure, compliance, anonymity or irreversibility claims.

Synthetic fixtures support software verification, not population evidence. Behavioral test matrices, data statements and datasheets should record generation intent, split, coverage, exclusions and failure modes ([Ribeiro et al., 2020](https://aclanthology.org/2020.acl-main.442/); [Bender & Friedman, 2018](https://aclanthology.org/Q18-1041/); [Gebru et al., 2021](https://doi.org/10.1145/3458723)). Repeated training on generated material can distort distributions, so future synthetic training—if ever approved—must preserve independent human-authored holdouts and cannot bootstrap claims from its own outputs ([Shumailov et al., 2024](https://doi.org/10.1038/s41586-024-07566-y)).

## Community-derived theme hypotheses

No Reddit post, YouTube comment, forum thread, support-group contribution, username, diagnosis, quote or raw page was collected or retained. Search-result discovery and support-organization resource categories were used only to identify these hypotheses for future participatory review:

- people may value explicit expectations about timing, channel and response windows;
- an editable clarification or repair prompt may be more useful than a “vibe verdict”;
- date format, sensory environment, travel distance and scheduling may be useful user-declared logistics;
- masking, stigma and disclosure control may affect trust and safety;
- online dating can offer controlled self-presentation while also creating misrepresentation, harassment and privacy risks;
- people may want space for long-form shared interests without having density treated as a deficit.

These are **not findings, labels or training targets**. AANE and CHADD resource pages show that relationships are an active support topic, while Ginapp et al. reports both benefits and risks in ADHD online communities; none licenses member-content collection or supports generalization to Berlin users ([AANE Dating & Relationships](https://aane.org/autism-info-faqs/library/dating-relationships/), accessed 2026-07-15; [CHADD relationship resources](https://chadd.org/attention-article/adhd-and-relationships-when-love-gets-lost-in-translation/), accessed 2026-07-15; [Ginapp et al., 2023](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ssmqr.2023.100223)). Platform content remains prohibited under `configs/training_source_registry.yml` pending source-specific contract, rights, privacy and ethics review.

## Matching policy derived from the evidence

The matching design is a deterministic comparison of **explicit, optional, user-controlled dimensions**, not a signal-inference model:

| Dimension | Allowed input | Unknown handling | Prohibited transformation |
|---|---|---|---|
| connection goal | explicit current selection | exclude from that filter or ask the user | infer commitment or relationship success |
| date logistics | explicit area, travel preference, date format | never default to compatible | infer income, disability or safety level |
| communication preferences | explicit channel, planning and response-window preferences | show “not provided” | infer diagnosis or personality |
| sensory/access needs | optional user-declared accommodations | private and non-ranking by default | infer neurotype or health status |
| boundaries and deal-breakers | explicit, reviewed finite values | fail closed for hard filters | infer from prose or behavior |
| interests | explicit tags, user-controlled | neutral | popularity or engagement ranking |

There is no total score. Each comparison preserves source, timestamp, visibility, provenance class (`explicit` or `unknown`) and a plain explanation. Fairness is multi-stakeholder and sociotechnical; offline parity metrics alone cannot resolve representation, exposure, safety or power concerns ([Deldjoo et al., 2024](https://doi.org/10.1007/s11257-023-09364-z); [Burke, Abdollahpouri & Mobasher, 2017](https://doi.org/10.1145/3079628.3079657); [Selbst et al., 2019](https://doi.org/10.1145/3287560.3287598)).

## Calm, trust-building and anti-compulsion design

The UI may use understated completion feedback—such as a stable checkmark after a user-controlled step—but must not optimize dopamine, time-on-app or return frequency. Attention-capture patterns, infinite feeds, auto-play, pull-to-refresh rewards, streaks, scarcity, read-receipt pressure, countdowns, escalating notifications and variable rewards are prohibited. The experience should be finite, dismissible, keyboard accessible, reduced-motion aware and useful in an abstention state. Research on attention-capture dark patterns and digital self-control tools supports prioritizing autonomy over engagement maximization ([Monge Roffarello & De Russis, 2022](https://doi.org/10.1145/3491101.3519829); [Lyngs et al., 2023](https://doi.org/10.1145/3571810)). WCAG 2.2 and EU deceptive-design guidance are design inputs, not compliance findings ([WCAG 2.2](https://www.w3.org/TR/WCAG22/); [EDPB Guidelines 03/2022](https://www.edpb.europa.eu/our-work-tools/our-documents/guidelines/guidelines-032022-deceptive-design-patterns-social-media_en)).

## Legal and ethical interpretation limits

- GDPR Article 9 is a restriction and governance trigger, not an anonymisation recipe or product authorization ([GDPR](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/eli/reg/2016/679/oj)).
- UrhG §60d has eligibility, purpose, access and retention conditions for scientific research; Clarity does not assume that a commercial product, public scrape or model-training plan qualifies ([UrhG §60d](https://www.gesetze-im-internet.de/urhg/__60d.html)).
- Consent to analyze a transcript is not consent to retain it, annotate it, train on it, analyze the other speaker, or publish a derivative.
- Public availability is not a rights basis. Reddit, YouTube, forums, blogs and support groups remain source-specific, contract-sensitive and high-risk.
- Legal, privacy, ethics, accessibility and community review remain separate gates. This research synthesis passes none of them.

## Open questions

1. Which communication tasks do Berlin adults actually want, and which feel pathologizing or intrusive?
2. How should a participant describe mixed-language, Euro-English and code-switch contexts without being normalized to a prestige dialect?
3. Which repair prompts preserve agency across different relationship stages and power dynamics?
4. What false-positive and abstention thresholds are acceptable for each cue and language context?
5. How should hard-filter unknowns balance safety, privacy and discoverability without hidden exposure bias?
6. Can a future participant protocol evaluate usefulness without collecting raw relationship text?
7. Which disabled features should remain permanently out of scope even if technically feasible?
