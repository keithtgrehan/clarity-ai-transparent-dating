# Berlin communication support: gated go-to-market plan

Status: strategy and experiment plan; no acquisition campaign or participant processing authorized
Owner: Keith Grehan
Reviewed: 2026-07-15

## Market wedge

The initial promise is practical and narrow: **review wording before a difficult dating message and leave with one editable next step**. Clarity does not grade a person or relationship. It describes a small number of features in text the user is authorized to submit, states what it cannot know, and lets the user ignore or rewrite the suggestion.

Primary early audience after the real-text gate: English-speaking adults dating in Berlin who value explicit communication, including ADHD, autistic and AuDHD communities without requiring disclosure or proof of diagnosis. The first task should be a self-authored draft review. Received-message interpretation has materially greater third-party privacy and over-inference risk and is a separate later gate.

Positioning language:

- “Make your meaning easier to see before you send.”
- “A few wording observations, one editable option, and clear limits.”
- “Your diagnosis is neither required nor inferred.”
- “The tool cannot tell you what another person feels, intends or will do.”

Avoid “decode,” “mind-reading,” “compatibility,” “confidence score,” “emotion detection,” “AI dating coach,” anonymity guarantees and peer-reviewed-model claims. The deterministic rules may be benchmarked later; the product itself is not validated clinical advice or relationship evidence.

## Conversion sequence

The requested commercial destination is direct, consented review of a user's own draft—not an unlimited synthetic-demo product. It cannot be enabled in the current branch. The gated future sequence is:

1. A concise landing explanation shows one fictional before/after example and the limits.
2. The user chooses a task before supplying content.
3. A just-in-time notice distinguishes their own draft from content received from another person.
4. The service displays a potential-identifier receipt and a redacted review state before analysis continues.
5. The result offers no more than three observations and one editable repair action.
6. The user can clear the session immediately; saving, history and training are separate, off-by-default choices if ever introduced.
7. A paid conversion may follow demonstrated usefulness; it must not be tied to urgency, streaks, response anxiety or variable rewards.

Terms and conditions acknowledge limitations and define permitted use, but they do not by themselves create a lawful basis, authorize third-party message processing, prove anonymisation, or validate the model. SG1 and the relevant GDPR/DPIA/security gates must pass before step 2 accepts real text.

## Offer and pricing hypotheses

| Offer | Hypothesis | Ethical constraint | Evidence needed |
|---|---|---|---|
| One draft review | Fastest proof of task value | No urgency timer or artificial scarcity | Comprehension, edit acceptance, clear-session success |
| Small monthly allowance | Predictable use without compulsion | No streak or loss framing; visible limits and pause | Willingness-to-pay and support load |
| Facilitator/coach workspace later | Professionals may value consistent bounded prompts | No surveillance, diagnosis or bulk client-data reuse | Separate controller/processor and professional-use review |

Do not optimize pricing around anxiety peaks or message volume. Test a transparent free allowance and a low fixed subscription only after SG1; do not use an infinite free-analysis loop as the acquisition mechanic.

## Launch phases

| Phase | Audience/data | Product surface | Exit evidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| A — current branch | Maintainers; fictional fixtures only | Local selector and closed deterministic result | Security/privacy tests and independent review |
| B — moderated usability research | Recruited adults under G4; no live service text unless protocol permits | Prototype comprehension and redaction walkthrough | Vocabulary, accessibility and adverse-finding report |
| C — own-draft private beta | Invited authenticated adults; consented self-authored drafts only | SG1 path, retention controls, incident support | Utility benchmark, subgroup/error review, deletion and incident drills |
| D — paid Berlin beta | Same bounded own-draft scope | Transparent allowance and support | Activation, paid conversion, safety/accessibility thresholds |
| E — additional inputs | Only after separate SG2/SG3 decisions | Received excerpts or audio independently gated | Third-party/audio assessments and owner go/no-go |

## Acquisition channels after G4 approval

- Partnerships with Berlin neurodiversity and accessibility organizations using reviewed, non-diagnostic language.
- Educational content about explicit requests, response windows and conversational repair, cited to primary research where appropriate.
- Facilitator-led workshops that teach the method without collecting participant messages.
- Search pages focused on tasks (“how to make a dating message clearer”) rather than diagnosis targeting.
- A referral invitation that is user-triggered and neutral, with no contact scraping, social graph upload or reward pressure.

Public community posts, YouTube comments, support groups and forums may inform aggregate hypotheses through a rights/ethics-reviewed research process. They are not a contact list, testimonial source or training corpus; do not retain usernames, quotes, posts, diagnoses or raw threads.

## Benchmark and trust plan

Before any efficacy copy, publish a versioned benchmark card covering:

- deterministic cue precision and false-positive review on original synthetic and separately approved evaluation cases;
- identifier-minimisation false negatives across US, UK, Euro-English and code-switching canaries;
- hard negatives for quotation, reported speech, negation, rejection and first-person obligation;
- subgroup and dialect error analysis without inferring a speaker's identity;
- abstention rate, low-signal behavior and repair-action editability;
- known limitations and changes between ruleset versions.

The claim is “benchmarked on the disclosed test set,” never “peer-reviewed model,” “understands autistic communication,” or “detects emotion.” External research supports design hypotheses; it does not validate the app's output.

## Funnel and decision metrics

Use quality and control metrics, not attention proxies:

- landing-to-task comprehension;
- proportion correctly identifying what the tool cannot infer;
- redaction-review completion and cancellation;
- cue usefulness and false-positive reports;
- repair action edited, ignored or used—not whether a message receives a reply;
- Clear Session success and deletion-request completion;
- accessibility task completion;
- unwanted disclosure, safety escalation and incident rates;
- trial-to-paid conversion and support burden after the gate opens.

Do not use time-in-app, streak continuation, reply speed, notification opens, message volume or emotional distress as optimization targets.

## First commercial experiment backlog

1. Validate the one-sentence promise and limitation comprehension with fictional examples.
2. Compare `structure_support` and `wording_support` task labels for clarity, not engagement.
3. Benchmark one-action results against cue-only results for user control and edit quality.
4. Test privacy-receipt comprehension and the ability to stop before analysis.
5. Price-test a small fixed allowance using static concept pages only after recruitment approval.
6. Run an accessibility and cognitive walkthrough before any real-text beta.

Current decision: **GO for local synthetic product review; NO GO for acquisition launch, real-text conversion, payment, private beta, public traffic, audio, training or production.**
