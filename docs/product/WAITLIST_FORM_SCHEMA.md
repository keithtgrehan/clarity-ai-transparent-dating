# Waitlist Form Schema Notes

## Purpose
Capture enough structured signal to:
- segment Berlin leads for interviews and pilot invites
- avoid a shallow “notify me” list with no operational value
- keep the form short enough for a landing page

## First-Pass Fields
- first name
- email
- city
- neighborhood
- languages
- neurotype context
- relationship intent
- communication needs
- source / referral
- interview opt-in
- pilot opt-in

## Why These Fields
- city and neighborhood help validate Berlin density
- languages matter in Berlin and affect comfort quickly
- relationship intent prevents broad low-signal signups
- communication needs reinforce the product promise from the first touchpoint
- interview opt-in helps founder research loop immediately

## Things Intentionally Not Asked Yet
- legal name
- diagnosis proof
- health history
- photos
- full profile content
- payment intent

## Review Notes
- test whether “neurotype context” wording feels respectful and clear
- verify whether relationship structure needs a separate question later
- consider whether district-level neighborhood input should become a select list after more research
