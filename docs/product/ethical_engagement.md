# Ethical engagement standard

This document defines target requirements, not current v1 behavior. Clarity must optimize for informed connection decisions, not attention before participant use; see `docs/control_room/RELEASE_GATES.md`.

- Candidate discovery must be finite and refresh timing explicit.
- No infinite swipe, streak, loot-box/intermittent reward, artificial countdown, scarcity claim, guilt, popularity badge, activity pressure, or reply-time penalty.
- Pausing, declining optional data, hiding fields, or disabling matching use must not reduce unrelated service access.
- Differences and unknowns use neutral language and are not defects.
- Notification and research consent are granular and reversible.
- User-facing reasons must reflect allowed declared evidence and disclose important missing inputs.
- Product metrics prioritize safety, accessibility, comprehension, unwanted exposure, equitable opportunity, and successful control use.

Any experiment that could influence exposure or user behavior requires a hypothesis, participant-risk review, no-dark-pattern check, rollback, finite duration, and release-gate approval.
