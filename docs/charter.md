# Clarity programme charter

Clarity exists to learn whether explicit, low-ambiguity product design can help neuroinclusive adults in Berlin form dating, friendship, and event connections with more agency and less social guesswork.

## Boundaries

- Berlin is the first bounded learning geography.
- Participation is 18+; current code is not an adequate age gate.
- Self-description is voluntary. No diagnosis or neurotype proof is required.
- Diagnosis is optional private research context only under separate consent.
- Dating, friendship, and events must become first-class modes before product claims say they exist.
- The system supports decisions; it does not predict romance or replace human judgment.

## Target participant benefit and power

Before participant use, users must control what they disclose, who may see it, and whether an eligible field affects matching. Unknown and different answers must not be treated as defects. The target design makes discovery finite, notifications quiet, and pausing unpenalized. Reports should create immediate user protection, while platform sanctions require accountable human decisions and appeal. None of these statements describes completed v1 controls; see `docs/control_room/RELEASE_GATES.md`.

## Non-goals

Clinical assessment, therapy, crisis response, diagnosis verification/inference, popularity or attractiveness ranking, engagement optimization, autonomous moderation, unconsented research/training, and a broad public launch are out of scope.

## Authority

Keith Grehan owns product and release decisions. Qualified privacy/legal review owns legal conclusions; named safety operations own moderation readiness; an independent reviewer owns release findings. No single implementation agent can waive a release gate.
