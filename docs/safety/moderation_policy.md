# Moderation and appeal policy draft

Status: policy foundation only; there is no operational moderator queue or participant-ready process.

## Categories

Harassment; sexual pressure; fetishisation; threats; hate or identity abuse; stalking; impersonation; coercion; scams; image abuse; privacy violations; repeated boundary violations; underage concerns; offline safety incidents; and other.

## Process direction

1. A user block takes effect immediately for that user without requiring a platform finding.
2. A report creates a separate case and minimizes evidence to what is necessary.
3. Automated rules may suggest category/severity and hold clearly defined content for review, but may not impose a platform sanction.
4. An authorized human reviews evidence, conflicts, context, policy version and prior relevant decisions.
5. The decision records reason, scope, duration, reviewer, policy version and evidence references without copying unnecessary raw text.
6. Affected users receive appropriate notice and a usable appeal route, subject to safety/privacy limits.
7. A different authorized reviewer handles appeal where practicable; outcomes and reversals are audited.

Immediate danger, credible threats, underage concerns, image abuse and offline incidents require a named escalation runbook, coverage and qualified legal/safety advice before beta. Clarity must not claim to provide emergency or crisis services.

Access, retention and disclosure for report evidence must be stricter than ordinary profile data. Reporting, blocking, pausing or appealing must not reduce a user's unrelated discovery opportunity.
